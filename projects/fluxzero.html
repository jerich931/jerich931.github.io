Flux Zero

An original design of a speed Rubikâ€™s Cube for use in official World Cube Association competitions with magnets introduced in the inner radius of the cube to decrease the moment of inertia, reducing the external force required to turn the cube at rates of over ten moves per second. Designed in Onshape and utilized FDM and SLA 3D printing additive manufacturing to produce prototypes and proof of concepts. Magnet technology is currently used in top flagship models for speed cubes on the market and used by tens of thousands of competitive Rubik's Cube speed solvers.

    Patent US10583351B2

There are 4 unique parts to the cube: the Corner Block(1), Edge Block(2), Center Block(3), and the Center Lid(3). There are 8 Corner Blocks, 12 Edge Blocks, 6 Center Blocks, and 6 Center Lids that make up the entire cube. Additionally, there are 6 screws(5), 6 compression springs(6), and 1 core(7) with 6 screw extrusions to hold each screw. Each screw fits through a Center Block and is separated by a compression spring and fits into the core as seen in Diagram 1.

There are 6 Center Blocks. There are 4 extrusions (Center Wings) on the base of a Corner Block which fit into each Edge Block as seen in Diagram 5. Each Center Lid(1) fits on into a Center Block(2) as seen in Diagram 2. A Center Lid has 1 face, and a sticker may be placed here.

There are 12 Edge Blocks, each having 2 faces(9). The Plate Lip(6) fits underneath the Center Block(2), and the Center Wing(3) fits into the Ridge(7) as seen in Diagram 5. The Edge Track(5) holds the Corner Block(12), which is held from the Corner Base(10). The Edge Holder(8) holds the Corner Base(10) and ensures stability of the Corner Block(12).

There are 8 Corner Blocks, each having 3 faces(11). The Anti-Twist Extrusions(9) prevent the Corner Block from twisting, which may result in an impossible state of the cube. The Corner Base(10) fits into the Edge Track(5) as seen in Diagram 6.

Magnets are placed in the cube to ensure further stability while each piece moves from each turn. 3 magnets are placed in the Corner Base(3) as shown in Diagram 12 and 2 magnets of the opposite polarity of the Corner Base magnets are placed in the Bottom Edge Face(3) shown in Diagram 13. In total, there are 48 magnets, 24 of one polarity, 24 of the opposite polarity. There are 3 magnets in each of the 8 Corner Blocks, and 2 opposite polarity magnets in each of the 12 Edge Blocks.
